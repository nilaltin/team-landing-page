1\. Team Purpose and Mission

**Purpose / Mission Statement:**  
*(Why this team exists and what it aims to achieve)*

Our team exists to deliver a user-friendly, informative Pacific
Northwest hiking web app that promotes safe and responsible outdoor
recreation, addressing trail overcrowding and providing reliable trail
information. We aim to collaborate effectively, support each other, and
produce a high-quality product meeting The Great Outdoors' objectives.

**Team Goals:**

*(Short-term and long-term goals, aligned with project success)*

- Short-term:

\- Develop and launch a functional MVP hiking web app within 2-4 months.

\- Establish clear communication channels and collaboration practices.

\- Complete key milestones (project planning, design, development,
testing) as scheduled.

- Long-term:

\- Foster a community of hikers and outdoor enthusiasts through the web
app.

\- Continuously improve the app based on user feedback.

\- Support The Great Outdoors' mission of promoting environmental
stewardship.

3\. Team Norms and Operating Guidelines

**Meeting Cadence:**

- Frequency (e.g., weekly, biweekly): Weekly

- Duration / Format (in-person, virtual, hybrid): Virtual Meetings

**Communication Guidelines:**

- Primary tools (e.g., email, Slack, Teams): 1‑hour virtual call via
  Discord

- Response time expectations: Reply within 24 hours

- File sharing / documentation: Shared Google Drive folder for
  documents.

**Behavioral Norms / Expectations:**

- Attendance: All members must attend meetings on time

- Participation: Active contribution in discussions and tasks

- Accountability / quality standards: Deliver tasks by agreed deadlines
  with quality checks

4\. Decision-Making Process

- **How decisions will be made (consensus, majority vote, leader
  decision):**

The primary decision‑making style is a leader decision (the Sponsor
makes the final call) with input from the Project Manager (a
collaborative/consultative approach).

- **Escalation process if consensus cannot be reached:** Team Lead →
  Project Manager → Project Sponsor

5\. Conflict Resolution Plan

- Steps to resolve conflicts within the team:

\- Identify and acknowledge the conflict

\- Discuss and attempt resolution among team members

\- Involve Project Manager for mediation if needed

\- Document the conflict and resolution process

Escalate to team lead or sponsor if unresolved: Project Sponsor (Evan
Holt)

6\. Key Resources / Support

- Tools, software, or platforms needed: Visual Studio Code, GitHub,
  Figma, Jira.

- Access / accounts / credentials: Shared GitHub repo and Google Drive
  credentials

- External support or stakeholders: Evan Holt (Project Sponsor), End
  Users (Hikers & Outdoor Enthusiasts)

7\. Signatures / Agreement

We, the undersigned, commit to follow this Team Charter and work
collaboratively to achieve the team’s goals.
